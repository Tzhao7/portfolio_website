Electric Boat Internship Experience

I have gained a considerable amount of practical experience interning at General Dynamics Electric Boat. During the summer of 2019, I created a project-specific TCP & UDP communication test tool that verifies messages between impressed current cathodic protection interfaces. The winter of that year, I created a GUI that communicates with shipboard amplifiers and displays their triaxial gains, current, and more, from which user can modify currents and log recorded data as needed. In addition, I worked on recreating the quantitative simulation portion of the Electric Plant Control Panel Model using MATLAB and Simulink. This past summer, I created a multi-threaded communication test tool for a functioning battery monitoring emulator, and created and integrated several modules that facilitate commmuncation between sensor and data acquisition modules for the Software Verification Platform. Given a set of different projects every internship, I have been exposed to a wide range of languages, software, and tools, some of which were learned on-the-go, while others I were familiar with. Unfortunately, because Electric Boat is a US government contractor, the work done required a secret clearance, meaning that physical code cannot leave company premises.

UConn Research Experience

I work as an undergraduate researcher under the ReTHi group of UConn's System Optimization Laboratory. Alongside several graduate students, I modeled several components of the Oxygen Generation Assembly (OGA) according to behavioral characteristics including nominal power consumption and thermal effects. These state-space analysis results will be run through regression analysis to optimize the individual components of the OGA. The work that we have done has been compiled as part of a future presentation to ReTHi advisers.
